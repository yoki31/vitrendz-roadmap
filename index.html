Things to remember

And, mistakes to avoid!

1. Never stop learning

Things in demand today might be outdated soon, stay updated


If you can invest in a Netflix subscription, you can also afford to buy a course on Udemy (It's cheaper)

2. BUT! Do not try to learn everything at once

Rome wasn't built in a day


1 hour daily for 20 days > 20 hours in one day

3. Prepare a proper learning strategy

Try to make a roadmap for the concepts you are going to learn, and the courses you are going to do


HTML - CSS (HTML and CSS by Jon Duckett)
JS (w3schools and freecodecamp)
ReactJS (TheLeanPRogrammer) - NodeJS (Udemy)

20:10 (Learning:Implementation) ratio?


Keep learning target?

4. Implement MORE, MORE, and MORE!

Online courses and certifications alone aren't going to get you anywhere

5. Don't make things complex

Stop using "trending words" or "trending libraries" just for the sake of it


Do things only if required

6. Production over perfection

Don't over-think


Don't over-engineer

7. REST

Don't forget to take breaks and sleep properly

8. TEACH

The more you teach, the more you learn


Mentor your juniors? Write tutorial blogs?

9. Replace "Perfection" by "Constant Learner"

No matter how much you know, you can never know everything


Have a student mindset throughout your life

10. Replace "Comparison" by "Inspiration

Instead of feeling overwhelmed, or envious, take inspiration from their hard work


Set goals to be at the position they are.

YOUR ROADMAP!

Form YOUR OWN 6-months roadmap -

  1. List down your dreams (Be as unreasonable as possible)
  2. Pick up the top 4-5 things that you want THE MOST!
  3. For each of them, write down the things you can do to achieve them
  4. Create a budget
  5. Assign a deadline for each item

BINGO! You have your roadmap ready!
